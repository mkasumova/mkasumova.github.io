What is digital accessibility?

Disability in the landscape of technology is often overlooked -- many disabled users may find themselves being held back by inaccessible websites, apps, and technology. From physical barriers, such as hard to handle technology, to inaccessible digital user experience like flashing lights on a website ad, there must be guidelines to help set a frame of reference for developers.

Founded by W3C, the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) were developed in 1995. These guidelines work to keep the internet inclusive, addressing solutions for inaccessibility on the web.

There are 3 levels of accessibility in the digital world:

Level A: The most basic level of web accessibility, including features such as alt text.

Level AA: The standard for websites – this is the level that is the most desirable and is legally protected.

Level AAA: The highest level of accessibility for websites. This level is not always achievable, so it is not an industry standard.

Creators in the digital landscape must stay intentional – in other words, those who create content on the internet must have purpose for their content decisions, and even more, keep their content up to par with accessible standards. These choices include:

  • • Captioning for videos

  • • Adequate color contrast

  • • Structured headings

  • • Alt text for image descriptions
